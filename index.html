Education

In a global pursuit of progress and equality, International Women’s Day serves as a poignant reminder of the continuous fight for gender balance. While advancements have been made, there remains a significant gap, particularly in education. Education stands as a cornerstone for empowerment, yet countless women worldwide continue to face barriers to accessing it. As we mark International Women's Day 2024, it is crucial to contemplate the profound impact education holds in crafting an equitable society. Education catalyses women's empowerment by providing them with the knowledge, skills, and confidence to assert their rights and pursue their aspirations. According to UNESCO, education is a fundamental human right and a key driver of sustainable development and gender equality. When educated, women are more likely to participate in the labour force, earn higher incomes, and make informed decisions about their health, family, and community. In the global context, progress towards gender equality has been promising but uneven. While enrollment rates for girls have increased significantly in many parts of the world, disparities persist, especially in regions affected by conflict, poverty, and cultural norms that discriminate against women. Moreover, women continue to be underrepresented in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) fields, limiting their opportunities for economic advancement and leadership roles. On the flip side, female enrollment in higher education has surged by 28% over the past decade in India. In STEM fields, women comprise 43% of the student population, ranking among the highest globally. These figures are mirrored in the rising presence of women in the workforce. In India, women face complex and multifaceted challenges in accessing quality education. Despite efforts to improve enrollment rates and literacy levels, gender disparities persist, particularly in rural areas and marginalised communities. Deep-rooted cultural norms, early marriage, lack of access to resources, and safety concerns often hinder girls' education. Additionally, the digital divide aggravates inequalities, with women in rural and remote areas having limited access to online learning resources. To narrow the gender disparity in education, collective action across all sectors is required. It is imperative for governments to maintain their commitment to investing in and implementing policies aimed at addressing socio-economic barriers that hinder girls' access to education. Civil society groups, academia, and the private sector also bear responsibility for championing gender-inclusive educational endeavours, offering scholarships, and fostering mentorship programs for girls and women. Promoting diversity and inclusion as fundamental principles within educational institutions is essential. A balanced representation of genders fosters a rich tapestry of perspectives, experiences, and ideas among students and faculty. We at QS I-GAUGE play a significant role in evaluating educational institutions based on diversity and inclusion, thus contributing to women's empowerment through quality assessment and rating processes. As we celebrate International Women's Day 2024, let us reaffirm our commitment to advancing women's rights and empowerment through education. Investing in women's education will unlock their potential and pave the way for a more inclusive and prosperous future for all.

Economic Participation

Women’s economic empowerment refers to women's ability to participate in markets, control productive resources and access decent work — as well as their autonomy over their time, bodies and lives. It also includes an increased voice for women, and meaningful involvement in economic decisions at all levels — from households to international institutions. Although almost 50% of the world's population is female, women continue to face a significantly uneven economic playing field. Astonishingly, 40% of the global female population still lacks access to formal financial services. Women are 20% less likely than men to have bank accounts, and 17% less likely to secure formal loans. Some 1.1 billion women are excluded from formal financial systems, which impacts economic growth. Global data also reveals that over 2.7 billion women confront legal barriers that constrain their job choices. Women aged 25 to 54 exhibit a labour force participation rate of 63%, significantly trailing behind men's 94%. On average, women’s income is only 77% of what men earn. This wage gap is further exacerbated by women’s disproportionate contribution to unpaid care and domestic work. Several global instruments are in place to promote and advocate for women’s economic empowerment. These include the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW), the Beijing Declaration and Platform for Action, and Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 5. Additional support comes from the International Labour Organization (ILO) Conventions, United Nations Guiding Principles, and the African Union’s (AU’s) Agenda 2023. Women's economic empowerment is also a central focus within UNDP's Six Signature Solutions. The UNDP’s Gender Equality Strategy 2022–2025 further identifies finance, digitization and innovation as pivotal facilitators of gender parity. Women are consistently underserved in accessing financial products and services. Traditional gender roles often confine women to unpaid care and informal labour sectors. In addition, deep-seated cultural norms may also reinforce gender stereotypes and constrain women's economic agency. To foster women's economic empowerment, strategies should involve confirming equal rights and opportunities, cultivating work-life balance, boosting access to assets and challenging societal norms. The UN Economic and Social Council advocates for comprehensive strategies aligned with SDGs. This includes eradicating discriminatory laws and promoting equitable distribution of resources through gender-responsive budgeting. The UN Capital Development Fund outlines a roadmap for equitable economies, where every woman achieves parity in access, agency and leadership. While this entails aspects like supporting women-owned enterprises, it also involves developing inclusive infrastructure and deploying public finance for gender equity. The economic empowerment of women, as a central pillar of the UNDP's Gender Equality Global Program, holds immense potential for building inclusive economies and societal transformation. Prioritizing women's economic empowerment not only fosters progress toward gender equality, but also acts as a catalyst for holistic development. Financial and digital inclusion are key. By providing access to banking, credit, insurance and savings, and enhancing digital literacy, women's economic participation can be substantially improved. Innovations such as mobile banking and fintech solutions play a pivotal role in bridging the financial gender gap. Encouraging women's entrepreneurship further amplifies the impact of financial inclusion efforts.

Political Participation

Our mission is to empower women in politics and leadership ensuring equal participation of women in women decision making, democratic governance, peacebuilding and in advancing WPS agenda. We believe this the only way towards sustainable peace, democracy, stability, and prosperity in our post-conflict region of the Western Balkans and beyond. Gender equality and the political empowerment of women are key elements for the consolidation of sustainable democracies worldwide. Women should be equal with men in deciding for a better future for all. Underrepresentation of women in politics, governments and parliaments continues, while empowerment processes show unsustainable, once more displayed by pandemics COVID-19. Women’s leadership and political participation are limited at both national and local levels, starting in political parties, leading positions, in elected office, the civil service to the private sector or academia. This despite their proven capacities as leaders and agents of change, their rights to equal participation in democratic governance and their significant legacy in peacemaking in times of wars in the Balkans. Despite some progress, we are far from real change. We are committed to change this reality and achieve gender equality. RWLSEE works on translation of gender and peace commitments into effective regional policies. We analyze the current status of women and mechanisms for fostering gender equality and women’s political participation and representation in decision-making; addresses the political and peacebuilding challenges situation in seven focus countries and entire region; identify specific challenges; and make policy recommendations on removing barriers and promoting gender equality and the political empowerment of women, and democratic governance and peacebuilding. RWLSEE presents policy recommendations and statements as an instrument to inform policymakers and relevant actors on what is the current situation of gender equality, WPS agenda and political and security situation in the region, what has been done in terms of the political empowerment of women, and what is expected to be done in order to successfully advance peacebuilding, democracy building and Sustainable development goals Goal 5 to achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls by 2030. Our platforms include international conferences, regional roundtables, political and inter-ethnic dialogues at regional, national, and local levels. They are specifically designed to address issues affecting women’s agenda, good governance and peacebuilding agenda in countries and region. We bringing together women politicians, members of parliaments, government officials and civil society representatives from across the Western Balkans and representatives of international organizations, UN Women and other UN agencies for sharing ideas and experiences from the region and best practices worldwide in support of mobilizing women to participate in politics and peacebuilding, amplifying regional women’s voice and perspectives and promoting regional cooperation

Social Inclusion

Women's empowerment in social inclusion is a transformative force that drives global progress, ensuring that women are not only participants but also leaders in shaping societies. Across different cultures and economies, women have historically been subjected to systemic exclusion from political, economic, and social spheres, limiting their ability to contribute to and benefit from societal advancements. However, as the world increasingly recognizes the critical role of gender equality in sustainable development, concerted efforts have been made to dismantle the barriers that hinder women's full participation. Education remains one of the most powerful tools for women's empowerment, as access to quality learning equips women with knowledge, skills, and confidence to make informed decisions about their futures. Societies that invest in girls' education witness significant improvements in economic growth, reduced child mortality, and overall community well-being. However, access to education alone is not sufficient; it must be coupled with economic opportunities that enable women to achieve financial independence. This includes access to fair wages, equal employment opportunities, leadership positions, and entrepreneurial support, all of which are essential in breaking the cycle of poverty and dependency. Additionally, legal frameworks play a crucial role in protecting women's rights, ensuring that laws do not discriminate against them but instead promote gender equity. In many parts of the world, women still face challenges such as unequal pay, limited inheritance rights, and barriers to property ownership, which impede their ability to achieve true financial security. Addressing these disparities requires bold legislative reforms and enforcement mechanisms that guarantee women's legal protection and access to justice. Social inclusion also involves dismantling cultural and societal norms that perpetuate discrimination and gender-based violence. Many women continue to face violence, harassment, and harmful traditional practices that strip them of their dignity and human rights. Governments, international organizations, and grassroots movements must work collectively to combat gender-based violence through policies, education, and community engagement. Additionally, reproductive rights and healthcare access are fundamental to women’s empowerment, allowing them to make informed choices about their bodies and futures. Without adequate healthcare, women face risks that hinder their ability to thrive personally and professionally. Political representation is another cornerstone of social inclusion, as women in leadership positions are more likely to advocate for policies that benefit marginalized groups and address issues such as childcare, family leave, and equal pay. Increasing the number of women in politics, business, and governance structures ensures diverse perspectives and inclusive decision-making processes. The global movement for gender equality has gained momentum, but challenges remain, requiring persistent advocacy, awareness, and policy changes to create a world where women and men have equal opportunities to succeed. The digital age presents new avenues for women’s empowerment, as technology and social media have provided platforms for women to amplify their voices, advocate for rights, and access new economic opportunities through remote work and digital entrepreneurship. However, the digital divide must also be addressed to ensure that all women, regardless of socioeconomic background, have access to technology and the skills needed to thrive in a digital economy. Achieving gender equality and social inclusion requires a collective effort from governments, businesses, civil society, and individuals, ensuring that policies and programs are not just symbolic but bring real change to the lives of women and girls.

Health & Safety

Health Empowerment Women’s health empowerment begins with access to comprehensive healthcare services, including maternal care, reproductive health, mental health, and disease prevention. In many parts of the world, women still struggle with limited access to healthcare facilities, lack of affordable treatment, and social stigmas surrounding reproductive health. Ensuring universal healthcare access is vital, as it allows women to make informed decisions about their bodies and future. One of the key aspects of women’s health is reproductive rights, which include access to contraception, family planning, and safe maternal care. The ability to control reproductive choices directly impacts women’s education, career opportunities, and overall economic independence. High maternal mortality rates, especially in developing countries, highlight the need for better healthcare infrastructure and trained medical professionals to ensure safe childbirth and postnatal care. Moreover, diseases that disproportionately affect women, such as breast and cervical cancer, require increased awareness, early detection programs, and affordable treatment options. Mental health is another crucial component of women's well-being, as they often face stress related to societal expectations, workplace pressures, domestic responsibilities, and gender-based violence. Women are more likely than men to experience anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorders, yet mental health services remain inaccessible or stigmatized in many societies. Providing mental health support, counseling, and awareness programs can help women cope with challenges and improve their quality of life. Safety Empowerment Safety is a fundamental right that allows women to live, work, and move freely without fear of violence or discrimination. Unfortunately, gender-based violence remains a global crisis, with millions of women experiencing domestic abuse, sexual harassment, and workplace exploitation. Strengthening laws, enforcing strict penalties, and creating safe spaces for women are essential steps in ensuring their protection. Workplace safety is another critical aspect of women’s empowerment. Many industries, particularly in developing economies, expose women to unsafe working conditions, including exposure to hazardous materials, lack of proper maternity leave, and unfair wages. Enforcing labor rights, implementing equal pay policies, and providing maternity benefits help create a more inclusive and fair work environment. Moreover, ensuring workplace policies against sexual harassment and discrimination empowers women to work with dignity and confidence. Public safety is also a concern, as women often face threats while commuting, traveling, or simply walking in their communities. Improved street lighting, women-friendly public transportation systems, and emergency response mechanisms can significantly enhance their sense of security. Additionally, education programs for both men and women on gender sensitivity and respect can contribute to changing societal attitudes and reducing violence against women. Empowering Women for a Healthier & Safer Future Empowering women in health and safety requires a multi-dimensional approach, involving governments, organizations, and communities. Investments in women’s healthcare systems, legal reforms for protection, and educational initiatives about health and rights can lead to sustainable progress. Women should have a voice in policy-making, ensuring that their health and safety concerns are addressed at the highest levels. When women are empowered with good health and a safe environment, they contribute more effectively to their families, economies, and societies. A world that prioritizes women's health and safety is a world that moves toward true gender equality, where women can thrive without fear or limitations. By creating inclusive policies and fostering a culture of respect and well-being, societies can ensure that every woman has the opportunity to live a healthy, safe, and fulfilling life.

Success Stories

1. Malala Yousafzai (Education & Activism) Malala, a Pakistani activist, was shot by the Taliban for advocating girls' education. She survived and went on to become the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She founded the Malala Fund, which continues to work toward education for girls worldwide.
2. Oprah Winfrey (Media & Philanthropy) Born into poverty and facing numerous challenges, Oprah overcame hardships to become a media mogul and one of the most influential women in the world. She built a media empire with The Oprah Winfrey Show and established the Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy for Girls in South Africa.
3. Indra Nooyi (Business & Leadership) An Indian-American businesswoman, Indra Nooyi served as the CEO of PepsiCo, making her one of the most powerful women in business. Under her leadership, PepsiCo saw immense growth and introduced healthier food and beverage options.
4. Kalpana Chawla (Space Exploration) Kalpana Chawla was the first woman of Indian origin to go to space. Despite challenges in a male-dominated field, she became a NASA astronaut and inspired millions before tragically losing her life in the Columbia Space Shuttle disaster in 2003.
5. Serena Williams (Sports & Tennis) Serena Williams broke records in tennis, winning 23 Grand Slam singles titles—the most by any player in the Open Era. She also advocated for women’s rights, equal pay in sports, and maternal health awareness.
6. Marie Curie (Science & Medicine) A two-time Nobel Prize winner, Marie Curie pioneered research on radioactivity and discovered radium and polonium. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains one of the most influential scientists in history.
7. Ada Lovelace (Technology & Computing) Often considered the first computer programmer, Ada Lovelace wrote the first algorithm intended to be executed by a machine. She laid the foundation for modern computing and remains a symbol of women’s contributions to STEM.
8. Mary Kom (Boxing & Sports) A six-time World Champion boxer from India, Mary Kom fought societal norms and financial hardships to become one of the greatest female boxers in history. She also supports women’s empowerment through sports.
9. Ruth Bader Ginsburg (Law & Justice) A former U.S. Supreme Court Justice, Ginsburg was a champion of gender equality and women’s rights. She played a crucial role in landmark legal cases that advanced women’s rights in the U.S.
10. Katherine Johnson (NASA & Mathematics) A brilliant mathematician, Katherine Johnson was essential to NASA’s early space missions, including calculating the trajectories for the Apollo moon landing. Her story was highlighted in the movie Hidden Figures.

Marketing

1. Marketing plays a pivotal role in women’s empowerment by shaping perceptions, amplifying voices, and driving meaningful societal change. Through strategic and inclusive campaigns, marketing helps challenge outdated gender norms, highlight female achievements, and create opportunities for women across industries. In an era where digital media has a massive influence, brands and organizations have a responsibility to use their platforms to advocate for gender equality and promote women-centric initiatives. Traditional advertising often reinforced stereotypes, portraying women in limited roles, but modern marketing is evolving to showcase diverse, strong, and successful women in various fields, from business and science to sports and leadership. This shift helps normalize female excellence and encourages young girls to aspire to careers and opportunities once considered out of reach. One of the most powerful tools in today’s marketing landscape is social media, which has revolutionized how women’s empowerment movements gain traction. Platforms like Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, and Twitter provide spaces where women’s stories can be shared, movements can be mobilized, and issues such as equal pay, workplace harassment, and women’s education can be discussed on a global scale. Brands that align their marketing strategies with these conversations build credibility and establish themselves as advocates for change rather than just profit-driven entities. Social media campaigns focusing on women’s rights, body positivity, mental health, and leadership serve not only to educate but also to inspire action. Hashtags like #WomenInBusiness, #GirlsWhoCode, and #SheMeansBusiness have helped amplify success stories of women breaking barriers in their fields, encouraging more participation and support. Another significant aspect of marketing for women’s empowerment is cause marketing, where brands integrate social responsibility into their business models. Many companies now launch campaigns where a portion of their profits goes toward women-focused causes, such as funding education for girls, supporting women entrepreneurs, or addressing gender-based violence. For example, Dove’s “Real Beauty” campaign redefined beauty standards and encouraged self-love, while Nike’s “Dream Crazier” campaign, featuring female athletes like Serena Williams, inspired women to push boundaries in sports. These initiatives not only strengthen brand loyalty but also contribute to long-term social impact. Content marketing also plays a vital role in empowering women by providing valuable information, success stories, and mentorship. Blog posts, podcasts, YouTube videos, and webinars can educate women on essential topics such as financial independence, leadership, digital skills, and entrepreneurship. Highlighting stories of successful women in business, technology, and activism can inspire others to pursue their goals with confidence. Moreover, offering free marketing resources, online courses, and networking opportunities to women entrepreneurs can help bridge the gap between ambition and success, equipping them with the tools needed to thrive in competitive industries.

Technology

Women’s empowerment in technology is a crucial step toward achieving gender equality and fostering innovation in the digital age. Technology provides women with opportunities for education, economic independence, leadership, and social advocacy. However, despite advancements, women still face challenges such as the digital divide, underrepresentation in STEM fields, gender bias in the workplace, and limited access to funding for tech startups. In many regions, cultural norms and economic barriers restrict women’s access to digital devices and the internet, preventing them from fully participating in the digital revolution. Bridging this gap requires government policies, corporate initiatives, and community programs that promote digital literacy, affordable technology, and internet accessibility. Encouraging more women to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) is also essential to increasing female representation in the tech industry. Organizations and educational institutions are offering coding boot camps, mentorship programs, and scholarships to support women in STEM, while tech companies are implementing diversity and inclusion programs to close the gender gap. Technology has also empowered women economically, allowing them to start businesses, work remotely, and engage in e-commerce through digital platforms. Women entrepreneurs leverage websites and social media to market their products and services globally, while freelancing platforms provide flexible job opportunities in fields such as programming, digital marketing, and graphic design. Financial technology (FinTech) solutions, including mobile banking and microfinance apps, have further enabled women to manage their finances independently and invest in their futures. Additionally, digital education has played a transformative role in skill development, with online learning platforms offering courses in programming, data science, artificial intelligence, and other tech-related fields. Women, even in remote areas, can now access high-quality education and training, helping them secure better jobs and career advancement opportunities. Beyond economic empowerment, technology has significantly improved women’s health and safety. Mobile health (mHealth) apps provide crucial information on reproductive health, pregnancy care, and disease prevention, giving women greater control over their well-being. Telemedicine services allow women to consult healthcare professionals without the need for travel, particularly benefiting those in rural areas with limited medical facilities. Safety technology, including mobile apps with emergency alerts and GPS tracking, has helped enhance women’s security in public spaces. Wearable technology and smart devices also contribute to personal safety, offering quick access to emergency assistance when needed. In addition to health and safety, technology has given women a powerful platform for leadership and advocacy. Social media and digital platforms have amplified women’s voices, allowing them to raise awareness on issues such as gender-based violence, workplace discrimination, and equal pay. Movements like #MeToo and #TimesUp have gained global recognition, demonstrating how technology can be harnessed to challenge systemic injustices and drive social change.
